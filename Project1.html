The moment you tell a dutch person you are coming to visit their country and ask how you can get around they will insist you use the 9292 app. But the app lacks vital features, feels outdated and is too complex, and therefore gives off this negative experience and foreigners question why do they not just use google maps.

My partner, Adam, & I decided upon ourselves to dig deeper and identify all flaws and find possible solutions to these underlying issues.

9292 has been the go-to app for many dutch citizens but with a growing number of users being frustrated due to lack of updates and support. There were a growing number of fundamental flaws. With the rapid growth in users and the last update in 2018, the 9292 app has become outdated and is now the predecessor of public transportation apps.

My Role

Adam & I are both first-year student in The Hague University of Applied Sciences. We both have had set our roles to work as a design team and work as partners throughout the whole design process.

Our Problem

At the start of the project, we knew there where major issues. So we both told each other to use the app for a set week to really get a feel for it and be a user of the app. We had no previous research no insights or any data to work on. We had to start from ground zero.

After our week of using the app, we came together and wrote all our whole experience, what we felt, what was good and bad and what we felt needed drastic improvement.

We wrote down all our initial thoughts and assumptions down.

Once we had all our necessary information we realised we needed too confirm our set assumptions and thoughts.

This is where our project has kept to a holt.
